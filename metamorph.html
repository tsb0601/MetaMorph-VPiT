Finding 1
Visual Generation Emerges Naturally From Understanding
We only need very small number of samples to unlock visual generation when co-training with understanding tasks.

Visual generation capability can be unlocked with significantly less data when co-trained with visual understanding tasks. Our experiments show that as little as 200K samples are sufficient when combined with understanding tasks, compared to millions needed for pure generation training.

200K
Samples needed with co-training vs. millions for traditional approaches
Finding 2
Visual Understanding and Generation are Synergistic
Better understanding leads to better generation and vice versa

There exists a strong correlation between visual understanding and generation capabilities. As models improve their understanding abilities, their generation capabilities naturally enhance, and vice versa, creating a powerful synergistic effect.

Strong Correlation
Between understanding and generation performance metrics
Finding 3
Understanding Data is Much More Effective for Both Understanding and Generation
Understanding data significantly improves both understanding and generation performance comparing to generation data.

Understanding data proves to be significantly more valuable than generation data. The impact of understanding data on both tasks suggests that focusing on understanding capabilities is a more efficient training strategy.

2-3×
Greater improvement from understanding data across all metrics
Finding 4
Visual Generation Aligns With Visually Demanding Understanding Tasks
Generation ability strongly correlates with visual understanding tasks but not with knowledge-based tasks

Visual generation capabilities show strong correlation with visually demanding tasks but weaker correlation with knowledge-based tasks. This suggests that visual understanding and generation share fundamental visual processing capabilities.

0.85+
Correlation with visual understanding tasks
